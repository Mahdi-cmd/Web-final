Extinct Insects

Poko Noctuid Moth

The poko noctuid moth was a moth in the family Noctuidae. The species was first described by Arthur Gardiner Butler in 1881. It is now an extinct species.

Agrotis Photophila

Agrotis photophila, the light-loving noctuid moth, was a species of moth in the family Noctuidae. It was endemic to Oʻahu, Hawaiʻi, United States.

Chestnut Casebearer

The chestnut casebearer moth (Coleophora leucochrysella) was a species of moth in the family Coleophoridae. It was endemic to the United States, where it was found in Indiana.

Heliogomphus Lyratus

Heliogomphus lyratus is a species of dragonfly in the family Gomphidae. It is endemic to Sri Lanka. Its natural habitats are subtropical or tropical moist lowland forests and rivers. It is threatened by habitat loss.

Alloperla Roberti

Alloperla roberti, the Illinois sawfly or Robert's stonefly, was a species of stonefly in the family Chloroperlidae. It was endemic to Illinois and its type locality was in Rock Island County. It has not been seen since 1860 and is likely extinct.

Eupropacris Abbreviata

Eupropacris abbreviata, commonly known as the Kilosa Noble Grasshopper, is a species of grasshopper of the family Acrididae. The species is endemic to Kilosa, Tanzania, and is now extinct due to deforestation.
